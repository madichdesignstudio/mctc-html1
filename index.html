Matthew McConaughey

Actor, storyteller, professor, and author of "Greenlights"

About Matthew McConaughey

Matthew McConaughey, born in Uvalde, Texas in 1969, rose to fame after his breakout role in Dazed and Confused, later becoming a well-known romantic comedy star before shifting into a major dramatic career during the "McConaissance" with films like Dallas Buyers Club, Mud, Interstellar, and True Detective. He also teaches at the University of Texas, wrote the bestselling memoir Greenlights, and co-founded the Just Keep Livin Foundation to support youth wellness programs.

Timeline of Key Moments

  • 1969: Matthew McConaughey is born in Uvalde, Texas, the youngest of three brothers in a working-class family that shaped his grounded and reflective worldview.
  • 1993: Lands his breakout role as Wooderson in Dazed and Confused, originally a small part that became iconic thanks to his ad-libbed lines and natural charisma.
  • 1996: Gains major recognition with his leading role in A Time to Kill, establishing him as a serious up-and-coming actor in Hollywood.
  • 2001–2009: Becomes one of the most recognizable faces in romantic comedies, starring in hits like The Wedding Planner, How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days, and Failure to Launch. This period builds his global fame but also typecasts him in a narrow lane.
  • 2011: Begins intentionally reshaping his career by turning down rom-com scripts and pursuing dramatic, independent, and character-driven films. This marks the start of the cultural shift later called the “McConaissance.”
  • 2012: Marries Camila Alves, a Brazilian model and entrepreneur. Together they build a private, family-centered life focused on philanthropy and creativity.
  • 2008–2012: Welcomes three children: Levi (2008), Vida (2010), and Livingston (2012). McConaughey has often shared that fatherhood is one of the most defining parts of his identity.
  • 2013: Wins the Academy Award for Best Actor for his transformative role as Ron Woodroof in Dallas Buyers Club, solidifying his shift into dramatic acting.
  • 2014: Stars as Rust Cohle in HBO’s True Detective, earning critical acclaim for one of the most memorable television performances of the decade.
  • 2020: Publishes his memoir Greenlights, which becomes a #1 bestseller and shifts his public persona toward philosopher-storyteller and life strategist.
  • 2020s: Continues teaching at the University of Texas, producing films, expanding philanthropic work through the Just Keep Livin Foundation, and exploring new creative ventures.
